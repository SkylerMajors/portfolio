Values

Knowledge

“Wisdom is not a product of schooling but of the lifelong attempt to acquire it.” ― Albert Einstein

To me, the constant pursuit of knowledge is one of the key foundations to my character. To believe that I have everything figured out and there is nothing more to learn on a subject, is severely limiting. By constantly expanding and ammending my current base of knowledge, I can best improve myself and how I understand and interact with others.

Service

"Being of service to others is what brings true happiness." -Marie Osmond

I have found in my journey to find true meaning in life that nothing is more fulfilling than being of service to others. Being able to help others gives me a healthy way to get outside of myself and create a lasting and meaningful impact on others' lives. My philosophy is that I should always try to leave people I meet better off than when I left them. By actively listening to people's stories and offering insightful and meaningful wisdom on their respective situations, I believe I can challenge others to strive to become better.

Excellence

"We are what we repeatedly do. Excellence, then, is not an act, but a habit." -Will Durant

Excellence is not an endpoint but a constant pursuit to become better. At the foundation of all my values the pursuit of excellence is the bedrock from which everything else is built. To be the best that I can possibly be at whatever I am set to do, is my goal and as a result it's imperative that I am constantly learning, adapting, and walking to the beat of my own drum. This unrelenting goal allows me to be an agent of change while also remaining changable myself.

Morality

"The individual has always had to struggle to keep from being overwhelmed by the tribe. If you try it, you will be lonely often, and sometimes frightened. But no price is too high to pay for the privilege of owning yourself." -Friedrich Nietzsche

I strongly believe that morality is a personal code of ethics that varries from person to person. Pulling from the philosophical texts of Nietzsche's "Thus Spoke Zarathustra" Higher Men will have a code of ethics or morality that is often distinctly different from that of the all consuming herd. While the herd's moral code may be sound and functional within the herd, it is not all encompassing. The same goes for the Higher Man's code, it is sutable for him but it may not be the measuringstick to impose upon the herd.
